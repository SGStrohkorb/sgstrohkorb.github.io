Engineering Graphics Instructor

Teaching a class is not an opportunity that many undergraduate students get to have. I was lucky enough to be hired as a ME 212 Engineering Graphics Instructor at Kansas State University for the Spring and Fall of 2018. Engineering Graphics is a lab that meets two times a week for three hour sessions each. The class teaches students the basics in hand drafting and SolidWorks, our CAD software. I was responsible for teaching one of those days, as well as grading the homework. I also participated in the creation of exams and their grading.

But this experience was much more than just teaching my students and grading. I learned how to prepare class content. I went over my slides before I got to class, several times. I went over the examples I wanted to do, to make sure I did them right in front of my students. I made sure I knew how to do the homework, so when they had questions, I could answer them.

Answering questions was one of the hardest parts of this job. SolidWorks is a great piece of software, but somehow my students would regularly come across error messages I had never seen before. Thus, I had to be able to diagnose their problem right there, because often times there were multiple people that had a question. So I got good at tracing through their process and found out where they went wrong. I also made myself available to my students through email and text, which worked out surprisingly well.

Instructing Engineering Graphics isn't just about the students either. We had weekly meetings with all of the instructors, most of whom are undergraduates. I learned how to interact with them in a professional manor, which really helped when some of them were not the easiest to work with. This taught me a lot about office dynamics and now not to usurp someone's power. I am glad I learned these skills now before my full time job after I graduate.

Overall, I am really glad I took this position when I did. It taught me many good skills as an instructor, but also as a person. I am no longer teaching due to my schedule and stress of instructing. I was putting many hours a week (up to 20) into that job and with my current course work, I can no longer instruct and have a life outside of school work.
